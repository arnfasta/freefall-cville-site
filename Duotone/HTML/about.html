Black Masala

SEP 2

Washington D.C.’s Black Masala blends funk, ska, big brass and Balkan music together to create an energetic, soulful, completely unique experience

“Black Masala seems to be name rolling off of everyone’s tongue in the last year, and for a good reason: they deliver one of the most explosive performances you will see. Black Masala...will make for a rip-roaring evening of worldly music that is sure to be a don’t miss event.” - D.C. Music Download Blog

The Judy Chops

SEP 9

As a soulful tonic of swing, blues, and rockabilly, The Judy Chops sing straight from the heart of Staunton, VA in a way that feels like drinking a cool glass of something strong. Having honed their chops on the road, averaging 150 shows a year, the Judy Chops were awarded “Best Local Roots Band” in 2013 at the inaugural Red Wing Roots Festival. They come to the Freefall stage with three studio albums under their belt, as well as a belly full of what sounds and feels like fire.

CVille Sabroso

SEP 16

Cville Sabroso is a celebration of Latin American music and folklife for all ages and people!

Head down to Ix Art Park on Saturday, September 16th from 12pm to 9pm to experience the music, food, and dance of several Latin American countries. Cville Sabroso is supported by many Charlottesville hallmarks: Sin Barreras, LUA Project, Cville Salsa Club, Ix Art Park, and 91.1 WTJU.

Mighty Joshua

SEP 21

Richmond based musician Mighty Joshua creates inspirational reggae music with upbeat rhythms for your soul and positive lyrics to bring a smile to your face.

Baaba Seth

SEP 30

Baaba Seth, since 1991, delivers a high energy dance-mix of original worldbeat music with an afro-funk-jazz-rock vibe. The joy in their music is both obvious and infectious during live performances, as reflected by the ecstatic dancing in the audience that results from the first drum call until the last note.

Sally Rose

OCT 6

Known around town as the “glitter rock queen,” Sally Rose blends crisp and hard-driven rock n’roll with her own flavor of sultry blues.

Being no stranger to C’ville, Sally has played at the Jefferson Theater and Sprint Pavilion, as well as stepping up to the stage at the regional music festival Lock’n. But don’t let the glitter fool you -- Sally Rose is as rough and tumble as they come.

Alligator

OCT 14

Charlottesville’s own eight-piece Grateful Dead cover band have mastered the early tunes of the Dead with a lively, contemporary spin. Listening to the band sounds just like dropping the needle into an old 45, but without the scratchy and warped disc. Just sit back and get lost in the sounds of the vintage Dead.

Oregon Hill Funk All Star Band

OCT 21

The All-Stars are a group of Richmond based musicians. The best and the most influential come together to play originally written funk influenced jams with heavy horns and driving drums. There is absolutely no way an audience can sit still while this band of heavies plays.

Roosevelt Dime

OCT 28

Hold on to your socks-- Roosevelt Dime might just knock ‘em off!

This Americana quartet embodies the rhythms of brotherhood. Eben, Andrew, Tony, and Craig hail from different states and bring the country together through musical unity. Catch them closing out the Freefall concert series on October 28th.
